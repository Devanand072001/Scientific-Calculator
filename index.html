Calculator Usage Instructions

This calculator is designed to follow order of operation and allow scientific calculation. In general usage, order of operation prioritizes functions over exponents and lacks true parentheticals. However, if a user needs a calculation to take place before a function, they can utilize the history menu. By clicking on << you can open up the expanded function pannels. The right hand pannel shows calculation history. The values in this history can be toggled between the previous problems and the previous answers. So, for example, you can calculate "sin(pi/2)" by inputing: "pi / 2". Then clear the calculator, press "sin:" and go to the answer history and double click the top answer (the most recent). This will move the solution to "pi / 2" into "sin:" and allow for proper calculation upon pressing equals for the solution.

Non-function operators perform in the order a user would likely expect, two numbers to an operator: ex "2 + 2". Functions have one number to an operator: ex "sin: 5". Replacement occurs as replacement starting with functions, then exponenets, then multiplication or division (left to right), and finally addition or subtraction (left to right).

Opening operators are not recognized, with the exception of functions. To swap sign (go from positive to negative or negative to positive) enter a number and then press the "+/-" key. Pressing it after an operator but before a number
